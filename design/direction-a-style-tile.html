01 · The idea

A lamplit study, open to the night sky

Nous already speaks this language on its landing page — Cormorant Garamond, DM Sans, a violet constellation. Direction A brings that identity inside. Dark mode is the study at night: warm ink-violet surfaces, ivory text like lamplight on paper. Light mode is the same room by morning: parchment, ink, deep violet. Titles open like chapters; wiki-links read as a scholar's marginalia. The chrome stays dense and quick — only the reading surface breathes.

02 · Color tokens

Two rooms, one palette

The complete --color-* contract, both modes — paste-ready for COLOR_SCHEMES. Warm hue throughout (violet leaning red, never navy); accents descend from the landing page's #7C4DFF.

03 · Typography

Cormorant Garamond speaks, DM Sans works

The display serif appears only where thought is named — page titles, notebook names, section headings, the wordmark. Everything operational is DM Sans; code and data are IBM Plex Mono. All three self-host as .woff2.
